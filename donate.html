Your Generosity
Changes Lives.

No donation is too small. Every shilling, every tin of food, every pair of donated school shoes reaches a real person in a real community that needs it. Give today and be part of the change.

Why It Matters

This Is the Most Direct Thing You Can Do

There are many ways to care about the world. You can follow the news. You can sign petitions. You can share social media posts about climate change, poverty, and injustice. All of these things have value. But none of them is as direct, as immediate, or as tangible as this: giving to someone who needs it.

When you donate to YHA, your money does not disappear into an administrative overhead. It does not pay for consultants or conference rooms. It goes directly into the communities we serve — as food on tables in children's homes, as gloves and refuse sacks for our clean-up volunteers, as school supplies in the hands of children who couldn't otherwise afford them.

The people we serve are not statistics. They are neighbours. They are children. They are families doing their best in circumstances they did not choose. Your donation acknowledges them. It says: I see you. I care. I am here.

"We make a living by what we get, but we make a life by what we give."
— Winston Churchill
Your Impact

What Your Money Actually Does

Transparency is sacred to us. Here is a direct translation of what different donation amounts achieve on the ground in Nairobi.

KES 500
Provides a full bag of groceries — rice, beans, and maize flour — for a child in a children's home for one week.
KES 1,000
Supplies a student with a full set of exercise books, pens, and basic stationery for a school term.
KES 2,500
Equips one volunteer with gloves, refuse sacks, and safety gear for three community clean-up days.
KES 5,000
Funds a full community education session for up to 40 children — materials, facilitation, and transport included.
KES 10,000
Sponsors a full day clean-up operation in one settlement — team costs, transport, and waste disposal for up to 20 volunteers.
KES 25,000+
Sponsors a major donation drive to a children's home — food, clothing, hygiene supplies, and recreational materials for 30+ children.
Give Money

Choose Your Payment Method

We accept donations through multiple channels. Choose whichever is most convenient for you — all options are secure, direct, and go straight to our operations.

M-Pesa
Kenya's most trusted mobile payment platform. Send money directly from your phone in under 60 seconds, anywhere in Kenya. Available to all Safaricom subscribers.
PayPal
Donate from anywhere in the world using your PayPal account or any major credit or debit card. Ideal for international supporters who want to give from outside Kenya.
Bank Transfer
Prefer a direct bank deposit? Transfer straight to our organisational account at your local or international bank. Perfect for larger corporate or institutional donations.

Your Donation is Safe

All payment details shared with YHA are used solely for the purpose of processing your donation. We do not store card numbers or sell data of any kind. Every donation is acknowledged within 24 hours by email or SMS, and we publish quarterly impact reports so you can see exactly where your money went. For large donations, we are happy to provide a formal donation receipt. Contact us at donate@younghumanitarians.org [Placeholder] for any queries.

Community Voices

Their Stories, in Their Words

These are not our words. They belong to the communities, donors, and volunteers whose lives have intersected with YHA's work.

Before YHA came, our street flooded every single time it rained heavily. The drain had been blocked for over a year. My sitting room flooded twice. After their clean-up, it hasn't flooded once. Something that simple. Something that took one afternoon. It changed everything for us on this street.

GM
Grace Mwangi
Resident, Mathare North

We run a small children's home with 28 kids. Government support is almost nothing. YHA has visited three times this year and each time they bring food and supplies. But more than the things they bring, the children see that the world outside cares about them. That is worth more than anything you can put in a bag.

RO
Ruth Ochieng
Director, Upendo Children's Home, Nairobi

I started donating via M-Pesa every month because a friend shared one of their clean-up photos. It's only a small amount, but knowing exactly what it does — reading their updates, seeing the before and after — makes me feel genuinely connected to something real. I've never felt that way about a donation before.

BK
Brian Kamau
Monthly Donor, Nairobi CBD
Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most often from donors and community members.

We publish quarterly impact reports on our social media pages and website, showing exactly what was done with donations received in that period — including photos, numbers of beneficiaries reached, and breakdown of expenditure. We also send personalised thank-you messages with a brief update to every donor who provides contact details. We have a strict zero-overhead policy on donations — every shilling goes to operations.

We are working towards formal registration as a non-profit organisation, at which point donations will qualify for tax deductions under Kenyan law. For corporate donors who require a formal acknowledgement letter or receipt for accounting purposes, please contact us at donate@younghumanitarians.org and we will arrange documentation.

Yes, absolutely. When making a donation, you can include a note (via M-Pesa account name, PayPal message, or email) specifying whether you would like your contribution directed towards environmental clean-ups, children's home outreach, or community education. We will honour that request wherever operationally possible and confirm the allocation to you.

Yes — we gratefully accept international donations via PayPal and bank transfer. If you are a Kenyan in the diaspora or an international supporter, PayPal is the easiest route. For larger donations from organisations or institutions outside Kenya, please contact us directly and we will provide wire transfer details. Currency conversion is handled automatically by PayPal.

Our most urgent and consistent needs are: dry food staples (rice, maize flour, beans, cooking oil), children's clothing in sizes 4–14 years, school stationery (exercise books, pens, rulers), hygiene items (soap, toothpaste, sanitary products), and school shoes. Blankets and bedding are especially needed during the cold season (June–August). Please ensure all donated items are clean and in usable condition.

We would love to have you join us! You can volunteer for our community clean-up days, join our education outreach team, help with logistics and coordination, or contribute skills like photography, design, social media, or writing. Reach out through our Contact page or send us a message on Instagram or WhatsApp. We'll get back to you within 24 hours.
