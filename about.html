About Us

Young People.
Real Purpose.

We are Young Humanitarians in Action — a grassroots movement of young Kenyans committed to environmental conservation, community upliftment, and the stubborn belief that ordinary people can do extraordinary things.

Who We Are

Born in Nairobi.
Built for the Community.

Young Humanitarians in Action — known simply as YHA — is a community-led organisation founded on the premise that social change does not require wealth, seniority, or institutional backing. It requires only the determination to show up and do the work.

We are a collective of young Nairobians: students, professionals, tradespeople, and neighbours who share a single conviction — that the state of our communities is not fixed. The polluted rivers can be cleaned. The blocked drains can be unclogged. The hungry children in homes across the city can be fed. And none of it requires waiting for someone else to act.

YHA grew out of the simple frustration of watching problems that are entirely solvable go unsolved because no one with authority seemed to care enough to solve them. So we decided to care. We decided to act. And what began as a small circle of committed young people has grown into a movement that touches lives every single week — in the settlements of Nairobi's most neglected neighbourhoods, in the children's homes that rely on kindness to function, and in the classrooms where the next generation is learning whether or not their environment matters.

We are proud to be Kenyan. We are proud to be young. And we are proud to do this work.

Our Foundation

What Drives Us

Behind everything YHA does are three anchoring commitments — a mission we pursue daily, a vision we are building towards, and a purpose that makes every moment of effort worthwhile.

Our Mission

To improve living conditions in underserved communities in Nairobi and beyond through direct environmental action, charitable outreach, and sustained community education — using the energy, creativity, and commitment of young people as our primary resource.

Our Vision

A Kenya where every community — regardless of income, location, or political visibility — lives in a clean, healthy, and dignified environment; where no child goes to bed hungry; and where the next generation inherits a country they are proud to inhabit.

Our Purpose

To prove — through consistent, visible, measurable action — that young people are not the leaders of tomorrow. We are the leaders of today. And today, we choose to make our world a little bit cleaner, a little bit kinder, and a little bit more just than it was yesterday.

"We do not have the luxury of waiting. The floods come every year. The children go hungry every day. The drains are blocked right now. So we act — right now, with what we have, where we are."

— YHA, Founding Principles

Pillar One

Environmental Service & Clean-Up Days

The relationship between environmental pollution and human suffering in Nairobi is direct and lethal. Polythene bags and litter blocking storm drains turn ordinary rainfall into flash floods that kill people and destroy livelihoods. Garbage piling in open spaces in informal settlements breeds disease. Open sewage mixed with standing waste water is a cholera outbreak waiting to happen.

YHA responds to this reality with the oldest, most effective environmental tool available to any community: showing up and cleaning. We organise regular community clean-up days in Nairobi's informal settlements and underserved neighbourhoods — mobilising volunteers, providing equipment, coordinating with local waste collection, and physically clearing the rubbish, litter, and plastic that formal systems leave behind.

Our clean-up operations focus especially on storm drain clearance — the most directly life-saving work we do. A clear drain means a flood that doesn't happen. A flood that doesn't happen means a family that stays in their home, a child who doesn't drown, a business that doesn't lose everything overnight.

  • Community clean-up days in targeted settlements, organised monthly
  • Storm drain clearing and plastic waste extraction
  • River and waterway debris removal in Nairobi catchment areas
  • Coordination with informal waste collectors and recyclers
  • Tree planting in degraded public spaces
  • Environmental audits of targeted communities before and after
Pillar Two

Charity Work & Children's Home Outreach

Across Nairobi, there are hundreds of children living in homes and shelters that survive on goodwill, private donations, and the extraordinary dedication of underpaid caretakers. These homes receive minimal government support. Many of the children in them have known nothing but loss: lost parents, lost homes, lost childhoods cut short by circumstance. What they have not lost — what they deserve to keep — is hope.

YHA's charity outreach programme visits children's homes across Nairobi on a regular basis, bringing with us food staples, clothing, school supplies, hygiene products, and the simple human warmth of a community that sees them and cares. We believe that charity, done right, is not about pity. It is about dignity. It is about saying to another human being: you matter, and we are here.

Beyond children's homes, our outreach extends to vulnerable families within the informal settlements we work in — elderly community members living alone, families recovering from flood damage, parents trying to feed children through periods of extreme hardship. We respond to need wherever we find it. We do not require applications, proof of poverty, or bureaucratic processes. We show up, we listen, and we help.

  • Regular donation drives to children's homes in Nairobi
  • Food packages: staples, fresh produce, and nutritional supplies
  • School supplies: exercise books, pens, uniforms, school shoes
  • Hygiene and sanitation kits for children and families
  • Clothing and bedding donations, sourced from community drives
  • Recreational activities and morale-building visits
  • Emergency response to families displaced by floods or fires

Pillar Three

Community Education & Environmental Awareness

Cleaning a street matters deeply. But unless the people who live on that street understand why it matters — and choose, from knowledge and conviction, not to litter — the street will return to its previous state within weeks. Sustainable environmental change requires changing minds, not just clearing rubbish.

YHA's community education programme takes us into schools, community centres, and open community spaces within Nairobi's informal settlements — specifically targeting children and young people, because they are both the most impressionable and the most powerful agents of change. A child who learns that throwing plastic into a river causes floods will grow up as an adult who doesn't throw plastic into rivers. That is generational impact.

Our education sessions cover the basics of waste management, the relationship between pollution and health, the science of how blocked drains cause flooding, and the profound difference that individual behaviour makes at a community scale. We make these sessions interactive, engaging, and age-appropriate — because information that bores doesn't change behaviour, and we are in the business of behaviour change.

  • Environmental education sessions in schools in Kibera, Mathare, Mukuru and beyond
  • Interactive waste-sorting and recycling workshops for children
  • Community awareness campaigns: posters, local radio, social media
  • "Clean Street Challenge" — youth-led community competitions
  • Training community youth leaders as environmental ambassadors
  • Partnership with local schools to embed environmental stewardship in curriculum
What We Stand For

Our Core Values

Everything YHA does is rooted in a set of values that are not aspirational — they are operational. They shape how we work, who we serve, and how we treat the people we meet.

Community First

Every decision we make is filtered through a single question: does this serve the community? We exist for the people we work among, not the other way around. Their needs, dignity, and voice come before everything else.

Action Over Words

We are allergic to meetings that produce nothing. YHA is, at its core, a doing organisation. We believe in showing up, getting our hands dirty, and measuring ourselves by what we have actually changed — not what we have discussed changing.

Dignity Always

We give, but we never condescend. We help, but we never pity. Every person we serve — child, elder, or family — is treated with the full respect and dignity that every human being deserves unconditionally.

Environmental Stewardship

We love this earth. We believe it is both our inheritance and our responsibility. Every piece of litter we collect, every drain we clear, every tree we plant is an act of love for the planet we share with 8 billion other people.

Youth-Led Innovation

Young people are not a demographic to be patronised — we are a force to be reckoned with. YHA is proudly youth-led, and we bring the energy, creativity, and lateral thinking of our generation to every problem we face.

Radical Accountability

We are accountable to the communities we serve, to our donors, and to ourselves. We document our work. We measure our impact. We report honestly — including on what doesn't work — because trust is the foundation of everything we do.
