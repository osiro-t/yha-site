Why Impact Documentation Matters

Numbers Are Not the Point.
People Are.

It would be easy to put large numbers on this page and call it a day. But at YHA, we are deeply conscious that behind every statistic is a real person — a child who went to bed with food in their stomach because we showed up, a family whose home didn't flood because we cleared a drain, a young person who chose not to litter because someone took the time to explain why it mattered.

This page exists not to impress you, but to be accountable to you. To the communities we serve. To the donors and supporters who make this work possible. Every number here represents a commitment we made and kept. Every story is true. Every photograph was taken in a real Nairobi community by one of our volunteers. This is our record — and we are proud of it, while knowing there is so much more still to be done.

Field Stories

Stories from the Ground

Each clean-up, each donation drive, each classroom session has a story behind it. These are some of the ones that stayed with us.

01

The Drain That Changed Mathare North

In December 2024, YHA volunteers received a message through our WhatsApp community group from a resident in Mathare North. The main storm drain on her street — a channel running through the heart of the settlement — had been fully blocked for over 14 months. Every time it rained, the street flooded. Residents had complained to the sub-county offices repeatedly. Nothing happened.

We arrived on a Saturday morning with 18 volunteers, four wheelbarrows, and enough refuse sacks to fill a small truck. What we extracted from that drain over the course of six hours was extraordinary: compacted layers of polythene, plastic bottles, food waste, and silt. The channel was almost entirely sealed. When we finally cleared the last section and water ran freely through for the first time in over a year, residents standing on the roadside began applauding. That street has not flooded since.

For us, that Saturday was not exceptional. It was a regular YHA day. But for the people who live on that street — for Grace, who told us her sitting room had flooded twice — it was the day something finally changed.

November 2024 — Charity

"They Didn't Just Bring Food. They Brought Joy."

Our November 2024 donation drive to Upendo Children's Home was our biggest yet. Thirty-one children received new school shoes, exercise books, two weeks of food supplies, and an afternoon of games with our volunteers. The home's director, Ruth, told us afterwards: "The children still talk about it. They drew pictures of the people who came." We went back in January.

February 2025 — Education

The Day 40 Children Learned Where Their Rubbish Goes

We visited a primary school in Kibera to run our environmental education session. We expected perhaps 20 children. Forty-three showed up. Using visuals, demonstrations, and an interactive storm drain model, we showed the children exactly how polythene bags travel from the street to the drain to the river — and what happens when too many of them get there. The teacher told us two weeks later that her class had started a litter-picking club. Unprompted. All on their own.

Our Journey

Where We Started. Where We Are.

Every movement has a beginning. This is ours — a timeline of the milestones that have defined YHA's journey from a small group of committed young people to an active force for change across Nairobi.

Year One — The Beginning

YHA is Founded

A small group of young people frustrated with the state of their neighbourhood decide to stop waiting for change and start making it. The first WhatsApp group is created. The first clean-up day is organised with borrowed gloves and supermarket refuse sacks. Six volunteers show up. They clear one street. It is enough to keep going.

First Year, Quarter Two

First Children's Home Visit

YHA makes its first visit to a children's home in Nairobi. The team arrives with food, clothing, and school supplies funded by personal contributions from the founding members. The response from staff and children is overwhelming. Charity outreach becomes a permanent pillar of what YHA does.

First Year, Quarter Four

Community Education Programme Launches

Following the success of clean-up operations, YHA volunteers begin conducting informal environmental education sessions in targeted schools within Nairobi's informal settlements. The first session, in a Mathare primary school, draws more children than expected. An education curriculum is developed from scratch.

Second Year

Expansion to Five Nairobi Settlements

Word of YHA's work spreads. Community leaders in Kibera, Mukuru, Pipeline, and Korogocho reach out requesting support. The volunteer base grows to over 40 active members. YHA formally documents its operations for the first time, beginning a culture of transparency and accountability that remains central to its identity.

2024 Flood Season Response

Emergency Flood Response Operations

During Nairobi's devastating 2024 long rains, YHA mobilises rapid-response clean-up teams in flood-affected settlements. Volunteers work alongside residents to clear debris, extract rubbish from waterways, and support displaced families. The response draws attention to YHA's work and brings in new volunteers, donors, and community partnerships.

Today

Growing Stronger — and Further

YHA continues its work across five Nairobi settlements, with plans to expand to additional communities. Over 500 children have been directly reached through charity and education. The volunteer team grows every month. And the work — the real, hands-on, community-level work — continues every weekend, in every settlement, with the same energy and commitment that started it all.

Accountability

We Publish Our Impact Reports

Every quarter, YHA publishes a full impact report — including a breakdown of donations received, operations conducted, communities served, and outcomes achieved. We believe in complete transparency. If you support our work, you deserve to see exactly where every shilling goes and every hour of volunteer time is spent.

Impact reports are shared on our social media platforms and are available by email to any donor who requests one. We also publish our quarterly reports on our website as PDF downloads when available. This is our commitment to you: we will always tell you the truth about what we are doing, what is working, and where we need to do better.
